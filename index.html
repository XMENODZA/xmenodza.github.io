A Few Accomplishments

Project: Detecting Neutral Hydrogen (HI) Gas in Dwarf Galaxy, R-127-1

The image above shows a 21cm wavelength radio observation. It’s cycling through different frequencies within an image cube, with the cyan circle highlighting the dwarf galaxy being observed. From my research, I discovered that this dwarf galaxy contains no significant HI gas, classifying it as a gas-poor dwarf galaxy—a rare phenomenon in the universe.

To learn more, feel free to check out my iPoster, which I presented at the AAS conference, or dive into my Master’s Thesis.

Outreach In The Community

In October 2023, I organized a community event for viewing the solar eclipse, providing free resources and educational materials. The event had a fantastic turnout, and participants gained insight into the science behind solar eclipses and how they occur.

In March 2024, a rare aurora borealis event was visible in San Jose. I captured photos of the phenomenon, allowing me to appear on local news to share my experience. I spoke about the significance of this event, not only for the scientific community but also for inspiring young students to pursue STEM.
